Iaorana Tatou!

A Beginner's Guide to Becoming Tahitian

Why hello there! Welcome to my fun page. Fun fact about me - I served my mission in Tahiti!

This page is dedicated to helping you live your life-long dream of learning the Tahitian way. Just you wait; when you show up in Tahiti, the natives will think you're their cousin from the other side of the island. Allow me to walk you through the basics.

  1. Basic Tahitian words:
    • Iaorana : Hello
    • Nana : Bye
    • Maa : Food
    • Tamaa : Eat
    • Parahi : Sit
    • Ta'oto : Sleep
    • Maitai : Good
    • Mauruuru : Thank you

  2. Basic Tahitian phrases:
    • Tamaa maitai : Eat well
    • Eaha to'oe huru? : How are you?
    • Haere mai : Come
    • Ta'oto maitai : Sleep well

  3. Common Tahitian cuisine:
    • Fafaru - fermented fish
    • Poisson cru - raw fish in coconut milk (see video below to learn how to make it)



    • Poulet citron - breaded chicken with citrus sauce
    • Banana poe - banana dessert kind of like bread pudding
    • Lentilles et riz - lentiles and rice; a very common, cheap meal
    • Pain du chocolat - chocolate baguettes; best when fresh from the bakery

  4. Important things to know about Tahiti:
    • If a stray dog comes after you, yell "Heish!" - works like magic
    • If a Tahitian calls you fat, they are complimenting you
    • When Tahitians raise their eyebrows at you, they are saying "yes"
    • Tahitians use palm leaves to make baskets, hats, and other cool accessories
    • Food is Tahitians' love language; when they offer you food, EAT IT!
    • Tahitians will give directions based on landmarks rather than streets
    • OPH is a certain style of house; when a Tahitian says an OPH house, they mean one of the houses with green metal roofs
    • Tahitians are only afraid of 3 things:
      1. Ghosts
      2. Centipedes
      3. Falling coconuts - why are they afraid of falling coconuts you might ask? Check out this chart!
Congratulations! You have reached the end of basic Tahitian training.
Hurry, you have a plane to catch!
